How the 10,000 tokens were allocated (by script)

Corpus mix (weights ) sets each language’s share of the budget. Hindi and Sanskrit share the Devanagari script, so they pool the same sub-vocabulary — merges learned from one help the other. English was weighted heaviest to force its ratio under the 1.2 ceiling.

Method. Text was extracted from the four uploaded Wikipedia PDFs, normalised (NFC; duplicated Indic matra artifacts from the PDF collapsed), and stripped of citation markers, URLs and the reference sections so only article prose remains. A single word-level BPE tokenizer (Whitespace pre-tokeniser, one shared 10,000-token vocabulary) was trained on all four languages at once. A word = a whitespace-separated token; the ratio X = total tokens ÷ total words over the full article prose (every word). Weights were tuned by search to hold English ≤ 1.2 while minimising the spread across languages.
